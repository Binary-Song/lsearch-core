Flibberflop zingled the quorpling snarf, while the wobbly greebles hummed a melodious plonk.
“Do you reckon the flumptious sprockets will ever tango with the jibble-jabbers?” asked a wandering blorf.
Meanwhile, the quantum zucchini pirouetted elegantly over the spaghetti of infinite sock puppets, leaving only a trail of polka-dotted giggleberries.

In the distance, a council of sentient marshmallows debated whether gravity should take a holiday, or if the moon deserved a monocle.
Suddenly, a verbose pancake recited Shakespeare to a confused collection of dust bunnies, causing several teapots to blush and a rogue trombone to cry softly.

Finally, in the great library of lost eyebrows, a lone pickle discovered the secret to time travel: singing backwards in binary while juggling invisible pancakes.
And everyone clapped. Or maybe they didn’t. No one was really sure.
